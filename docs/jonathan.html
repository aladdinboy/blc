Perky Otter Jonathan

You only knew Jonathan through your local gym. He was a Tuesdays and Fridays regular, and always very friendly. You had chatted with him a fair amount, often when switching from cardio to resistance training. But nothing beyond that casual chat. So it came as a surprise to you when he invited you over to his place one evening.

"Are you sure?" You had asked. "I'd hate to impose."

He chuckled, wiping a drop of musky sweat from his ginger-brown beard. "Not at all. My rosemary bush in my back yard has had a growth spurt, so I've been looking for excuses to fire up the barbecue and grill up some rosemary flank steak."

So here you are now, in front of Jonathan's modest flat in the suburbs. He greets you at the door, perky and beaming and dressed in a soft plaid flannel shirt. The night air is, after all, a little nippy.

He guides you through his house, and you get impressions of rich black oak and the lingering scent of cloves, but before you can investigate any further, you find yourself on his back patio, lit with soft warm fairy lights and a couple candles. The grill is already sizzling, and Jonathan offers you a slice of buttered currant bread while you wait.

"You know, if I didn't know better, I'd say you were trying to woo me," you mutter, sitting down and taking the offered glass of wine.

"Maybe I am. Is it working?" He asks, a sparkle of mischief in his eyes.

You grin back. "I think it just might be."
